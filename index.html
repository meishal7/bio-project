Bioengineering

— Bioengineering is the application of the life sciences, physical sciences, mathematics and engineering principles to define and solve problems in biology, medicine, health care and other fields. In general, biological engineers attempt to either mimic biological systems to create products, or to modify and control biological systems.

Bioengineering Application Examples

  • Pharmaceutical drugs
  • Tissue-engineered organs
  • Medical imaging technology
  • Portable and rapid disease diagnostic devices

Cultured meat

Cultured meat

— Cultured meat is the form of cellular agriculture. Cultured meat is produced using tissue engineering techniques. It's created by painlessly harvesting muscle cells from a living cow. Scientists then feed and nurture the cell so they multiply to create muscle tissue, which is the main component of the meat we eat.

  • Making 1000 kg of cultured meat takes 45 to 78% less energy, that means 78 to 96% lower Greenhouse emission, about 90% less water and 99% less land
  • Not having to slaughter hundreds of cows for food
  • You will have less outbreaks of infections like e-coli and salmonella, which often people get by feces left in the meat processing plant
  • It has less saturated fat, and scientists can pick and choose proteins that will be grown, which means people can live longer

Plant-based meat

Plant-based meat

— The term “plant-based” refers to products made from plants that are alternatives to animal-based products. This includes plant-based meat, seafood, eggs, and dairy. Animal meat is primarily muscle tissue. Though plants don’t have muscles, they do contain protein, fat, vitamins, minerals, and water. The general method used to produce plant-based meat involves three primary steps. First, we grow crops as a source of raw materials. Second, we process these crops to get rid of the parts of the plants we don’t want. At this stage, we end up with the proteins, fats, and fiber ingredients that will become our plant-based meat product. Finally, we put together the desired mixture of ingredients. This ingredient mixture then goes through a manufacturing process to create the muscle-like texture needed for meat.

  • Less red meat means lower risk of heart disease
  • Can help reduce world hunger
  • Will not hurt the environment
  • Will give farmers their jobs so that they grow crops that regenerate landscape, and dedicate their base to growing crops going to plant-based food
